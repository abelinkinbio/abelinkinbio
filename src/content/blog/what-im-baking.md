# A Few Good Recipes 🧁

**Two muffin recipes I've been working on and what I've learned so far**

I've always wanted to open a coffee shop. Not in a serious, business-plan-on-a-napkin kind of way. More like a recurring daydream that shows up whenever I walk into a place with good lighting and a short menu. And one thing I always come back to in that daydream is that I'd want to have a few original recipes. Not a full bakery case. Just a few good ones.

So when paternity leave gave me a stretch of time at home — and a newfound respect for how much work goes into feeding people — I figured I'd start there. I grew up in the South where cooking was just part of the deal. My mom and aunt homeschooled us, and they cooked almost every meal. My aunt and uncles were always swapping recipes. I never really appreciated how much effort that was until I had a kid of my own and realized that just keeping everyone fed is a legitimate accomplishment. Cooking something that's actually good? That's a whole other level.

I started with muffins. Two specifically: a blueberry muffin with lemon glaze and a banana chocolate chip with a crunchy top.

The blueberry lemon one came first. I'd had a version of something similar at a café here in Lisbon and thought, "I could probably figure this out." Famous last words. The first batch was fine. Honestly, none of them were ever bad — they just kept getting better. The first thing I focused on was shape. I wanted them to actually look like muffins you'd see in a bakery, not flat little hockey pucks. Then came the dome. Getting that nice rounded top is weirdly satisfying when you finally nail it. Once I had the structure down, I started working on flavor — how much lemon zest is too much, how thick the glaze should be, whether to fold the blueberries in gently or just dump them. Gentle fold, by the way. Always gentle fold.

The banana chocolate chip was a different challenge. I knew I wanted a crunchy top — that streusel-style crumble that gives you some texture contrast against the soft banana interior. Getting the crunch right took a few tries. Too fine and it just dissolves. Too chunky and it falls off. But once I landed on the right ratio, it became my favorite of the two. There's something about that first bite when you get the crunch and then hit the warm banana and melted chocolate underneath. It's a good muffin.

Across both recipes, the hardest part has been the same few things: getting the right lift so they rise properly, getting that crunch on top, and — this sounds dumb but it's real — getting them out of the pan without destroying them. I've lost a few good muffins to a poorly greased tin. It's heartbreaking every time.

One of the unexpected joys of this whole thing has been trading recipes with people. It reminds me of my aunt and uncles swapping notes on what worked and what didn't. There's something nice about that. You send someone a recipe, they try it, they tell you what they changed, and suddenly you've got a new idea to test.

I'm still working on both of them, honestly. The thing I haven't cracked yet is longevity — how to make them so they still taste good more than 48 hours later. Right now, they're best fresh out of the oven or within a day. After that, they start to lose something. But that's the fun part. They're not done yet. Neither am I.

Happy baking ✌️
